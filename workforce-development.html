The Kingman Economic Region comprises eight zip codes that encompass 2,133 square miles, which contained about 29,726 people in 2019. This region has the highest population density of the three geographies detailed in this report at 13.94 persons per square mile, who compose a total of 11,843 households, with a household size of 2.51. The Kingman poverty rate is 14.3%.

Approximately 38.3% of these households earn between $15,000 to $50,000. Total employment in this economic region is 11,014 persons, with an average income of $27,205. The Kingman Economic Region's significant employers include Kingman Regional Medical Center, American Woodmark Corp., IWX Motor Freight, McKee Foods, Trueserve, Laron, Cantex, and Cascades Tissue.

This economic region contains the most diverse of the three and hosts significant manufacturing, transportation, logistics, aviation industries, and healthcare and tourism industries. The Kingman Economic Region must take advantage of its existing critical mass of sectors and continue developing its multimodal transportation options.

As ports on the west coast continue to experience more unloading delays, the freight hauling and logistics industries will be looking for alternatives further and further inland, since unloading delays cause massive monetary losses for these industries. Attracting sectors that focus on alternative energy component manufacturing (as opposed to generation) such as solar cells, is also worthy of attention.

The leading industries for employment in the Kingman Economic Region are Educational services, and health care and social assistance (28.3%) Retail trade (12.4%) Arts, entertainment, and recreation, and accommodation and food services(12.3%) .

According to the BLS, the most recent unemployment rate for Kingman is 7.2% in February 2021. For the same month, the metro unemployment rate was 0.3% higher than the Arizona rate. The unemployment rate in Kingman peaked in April 2020 at 17.6% and is now 10.4% lower.

5.1.2 Lake Havasu Economic Region

The Lake Havasu City Economic Region comprises four zip codes that encompass 2,396 square miles, which contained 54,495 people in 2019. This region hosts 22.74 persons per square mile, comprises 24,448 households, with a household size of 2.23. Approximately 36.2% of these households earn between $15,000 to $50,000. In Lake Havasu City, 10.3% of people live in poverty.

Total employment in this economic region is 21,187 persons, with an average income of $32,160. The major employers in the Lake Havasu City Economic Region include London Bridge Resort, Havasu Samaritan Regional Hospital, Mohave Community College, Sterilite Corporation, and Wal‐Mart. This economic region contains several cluster families and is more diversified than its neighbor to the north, Bullhead City.

ASU@Lake Havasu City is a small, low-cost extension of the Arizona State University system offering high-demand, undergraduate degrees. The campus opened in Lake Havasu City, Arizona, in the Fall of 2012 and focuses on an experiential, student-centered approach to learning, including information technology and healthcare.

The Lake Havasu Economic Region must build on its unique assets in the region as a hub for motorsports and information technology industries and nurture its well-established tourism industries.

The top three clusters in Lake Havasu City Economic Region include Educational services, and health care and social assistance, Retail trade, Arts, entertainment, and recreation, and accommodation and food services, which contain percent, percent, percent, of total employment.

According to the BLS, the most recent unemployment rate for Lake Havasu City (LHC) is 6.9% in February 2021. For the same month, the metro LHC unemployment rate was 0.0% lower than the Arizona rate. The unemployment rate in Lake Havasu City peaked in April 2020 at 22.3% and is now 15.4% lower.

5.1.3 Bullhead City Economic Region

The Bullhead City Economic Region comprises twelve zip codes from Mohave County, Arizona, Clark County, Nevada, and San Bernardino County, California. This region encompasses 2,591 square miles and contains 29,726 people in 2019. These statistics translate to 15.54 persons per square mile, who compose a total of 17,138 households, with a household size of 2.35.

Approximately 58 percent of these households earn between $15,000 to $50,000. Total employment in this economic region is 14,543 persons, with an average income of $24,274, and a poverty rate of 18.4%. The major employers in the Bullhead City Economic Region include Western Arizona Regional Medical Center, Silver Ridge Village, DOT Foods, and Mohave Community College.

This economic region is primarily reliant on the tourism industry, specifically the entertainment and gaming industries located in Laughlin, Nevada. Proximity to Lake Mohave and Lake Havasu provides a significant recreational sector, while the regional medical center is the core of this region's healthcare industry. The Bullhead City Economic Region needs diversification and should seek to support its neighbors' more diverse economies. Industry clusters that are mature in this region include Arts and Entertainment, and Healthcare.

According to the BLS, the most recent unemployment rate for Bullhead City is 8.6% in February 2021. For the same month, the metro Bullhead City unemployment rate was 1.7% higher than the Arizona rate. The unemployment rate in Bullhead City peaked in April 2020 at 22.0% and is now 13.4% lower.

5.2 Greater Yuma County (including La Paz County) Economic Region

High unemployment remains an issue in Yuma. Citing August 2019 data, the Bureau of Labor Statistics ranked Yuma as #1 among the 13 U.S. cities that post unemployment rates above 15 percent. Yuma's agricultural workforce, which adjusts to the picking season, is cited as the reason for the high unemployment by the Arizona Department of Commerce. Yuma County is 5,519 sqaure miles in area and has a population of 209,468 people, and a population density of 37.95 people per sqaure mile. Yuma county has a total of 73,098 households, with an average household size of 2.87.

La Paz County is 5,519 sqaure miles in area and has a population of 20,793 people, and a population density of 4.61 people per sqaure mile. La Paz County has a total of 9,346 households, with an average household size of 2.22.

Yuma County has always been seen as a lower wage area. Yuma County also chronically has one of the highest unemployment rates in the country, approaching a high of 30 percent at one time (August 2010). But many of the unemployed lack the training, skills, and certification to fill the better-paying jobs that are available. Companies with the need for more skilled labor come to job fairs, only to leave empty-handed because they can't find qualified applicants.

Not only do available jobs go wanting for lack of qualified applicants, but it also means the area has trouble attracting companies that offer better-paying jobs. Yuma County continues to be the destination for the lower-paying jobs. The area must have to have a higher-skilled workforce to attract better-paying jobs. On the other hand, the lack of higher-skilled jobs drives young people to leave the community to seek careers elsewhere. And others who come to Yuma as the military spouses and professionals often cannot find a job here to match their skills.

In Yuma County, 41.1% of households earn between $15,000 to $50,000, 75,903 persons employed total, an average per-capita income of $21,758, and 19.4% of people living below the poverty level. For La Paz County, 41.8% of households earn between $15,000 to $50,000, a total employment of 6,160 persons, and an average per-capita income of $22,879. The poverty rate in La Paz County is 22.4%.

The community has made some strides, such as the STEAM (science, technology, engineering, agriculture, and math) program offered by Yuma Union High School District, in partnership with Arizona Western College. In collaboration with the University of Arizona, the college is also making it easier for Yumans to pursue college degrees in engineering.

The top ten employers in Yuma County based on number of employees are: #1 U.S. Army Proving Ground (YPG) (military); #2 Yuma Regional Medical Center (healthcare); #3 Yuma Elementary School District (education) #4 Wal-Mart Stores (retail); #5 City of Yuma (government); #6 Yuma County (government); #7 U.S. Marine Corps Air Station (MCAS) (military); #8 Bose Corporation (manufacturing(; #9 US Border Patrol (government); and #10 ACT, Advanced Call Center Technologies (call center.)

The unemployment rate in Yuma County is 11.8% as of February 2021, with job growth of - 0.42%. Future job growth over the next ten years is predicted to be 15.46%. For the same month, Yuma County's unemployment rate is 4.9% higher than the Arizona rate. The La Paz County unemployment rate is 6.9% as of February 2021. Compared to the rest of Arizona, La Paz County's unemployment rate is 0.0% lower.
